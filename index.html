First What Does AYP and SRH Stand For?

AYP stands for Adolescent and Youth people and SRH stands for Sexual Reproductive Health

Why networking with AYP to connect them to SRH...?

Adolescence is defined as a period of transition between childhood and adulthood, covering ages between 10 and 19 years, is a unique stage of human development. It is characterised by physiological, emotional and sexual development, shaping a sense of identity and individual autonomy. For this Adolescents and young people, typically defined as individuals between the ages of 10 and 24, face unique challenges in accessing SRH services due to factors like social norms, lack of information, and limited access to healthcare.

    What are the factors contributing to AYP's not been able to access SRH services?

  • Lack of knowledge and awareness: Many AYP may not be aware of the available SRH services, their benefits, or how to access them.
  • Inadequate facilities and resources: Shortages of trained staff, limited availability of supplies, and long distances to health facilities can create barriers to access, especially for those in rural or remote areas.
  • Unsupportive healthcare environments: Facilities that lack privacy and confidentiality, or that are not youth-friendly, can deter AYP from seeking services.
  • Policy and regulatory barriers: Requirements for parental consent for certain services can limit access, particularly for older adolescents who may not want to involve their parents.
  • Economic barriers: Out-of-pocket expenses for services, transportation costs, and lost income due to seeking care can be prohibitive, particularly for low-income individuals.
  • Limited peer support: If AYP lack peer support networks that promote SRH knowledge and service utilization, they may be less likely to seek help.
  • Unsupportive family dynamics: Lack of open communication with parents or guardians about SRH can hinder access to services, especially if parents disapprove of or are unaware of the importance of SRH.
  • Fear of judgment and stigma AYP may be hesitant to seek services due to fear of being judged by healthcare providers, family members, or the community.
